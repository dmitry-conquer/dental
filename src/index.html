What Are Dental Implants?

The Dental implant procedure involves a titanium post (implant), an abutment, and an artificial tooth (prosthesis), either a dental crown, bridge, or denture. These implant posts are surgically inserted into the jaw bone, acting as the root of the replacement tooth. This unique approach to dentistry offers numerous benefits over traditional full or partial dentures and bridges.

The success rate of dental implant procedures is quite high. According to the American Association of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeons (AAOMS), dental implants have a success rate of 95% when placed by a dental specialist. The longevity and effectiveness of dental implants largely depend on the patient's oral hygiene, overall health, and lifestyle habits. Regular checkups with the dentist and maintaining good oral hygiene can contribute significantly to the success and longevity of the implant.

Why Choose Cosmetic Dental Implants?

Natural Appearance

One of the significant benefits of cosmetic dental implants is that they mimic the look and feel of your original teeth. This authentic appearance helps boost your self-confidence and gives you an attractive, flawless smile.

Help Preserve Facial Structure

Missing teeth can eventually cause the face to sag or appear sunken due to bone loss. However, with dental implants, the underlying bone structure is preserved, maintaining facial contours and preventing premature aging.

Improved Oral Function

Dental implants function like real teeth, allowing you to eat, talk, and laugh without discomfort or restriction. They are firmly anchored in place, so you don't need to worry about them slipping or causing embarrassment. It's also easier to maintain oral hygiene with dental implants than other tooth replacement options. This eliminates the risk of decay or infection that can easily occur with traditional bridges or dentures.

Long Lasting

Unlike other tooth replacement options, dental implants last up to 10 to 25 years or more with proper care. This makes them a long-term investment that can save you money in the long run. Traditional dentures may require frequent replacements or adjustments, adding to significant costs over time.

Preserve Natural Teeth

Unlike traditional bridges, placing dental implants does not involve grinding down the adjacent healthy teeth for support. This helps preserve the integrity of your remaining natural teeth and prevents future dental problems.

Improved Quality of Life

Missing teeth can significantly impact your quality of life, affecting everything from your appearance to your ability to eat and speak comfortably. With cosmetic dental implants, you can regain a full, healthy smile and restore your confidence in social situations. This can lead to improved self-esteem and overall well-being.

Process of Getting Dental Implants

Getting dental implants at Absolute Smile involves several stages, each contributing to your implants' overall success and longevity.

1. Initial Consultation and Assessment

Your journey towards a healthier smile begins with an initial consultation with our dental implant dentists. During this appointment, they will:

Assess your oral health individually.
Consider factors such as your medical history, the condition of your gum tissues, and your jaw bone density.
Based on these factors, they will determine the type of implant that is most suitable for you.

2. Implant Placement

Once it's determined that dental implants are a suitable treatment option for you, the next step is the surgical procedure.

Here's what happens:
The procedure is performed in our state-of-the-art dental practice, equipped with the latest technology.
The implant, essentially a small titanium post, is surgically placed into the jawbone where the missing normal tooth was.

3. Healing Process

Following the surgical procedure, the healing process begins. This is a crucial phase where:

The procedure is performed in our state-of-the-art dental practice, equipped with the latest technology.
The implant, essentially a small titanium post, is surgically placed into the jawbone where the missing normal tooth was.

4. Abutment Placement

Once the implant has successfully fused with the bone, the next step is to place an abutment. This small connector post attaches to the implant and serves as a support for the prosthetic tooth.

Here's what happens during this stage:
The dental implant dentist will make a small incision in your gum tissue to expose the top of the implant.
They will then place the abutment on top of the implant.
The gum tissue is then stitched back up, leaving the abutment visible above the gum line.

5. Final Restoration

The final stage of getting dental implants involves attaching the prosthetic tooth or teeth to the abutment(s). This restoration can take several forms, depending on your unique needs:

A Single Crown: If you're missing one tooth, a custom-made porcelain crown will be secured to the abutment.
Implant-Supported Bridge: This option is suitable if you have multiple missing teeth. The bridge is secured to two or more adjacent implants.
Dentures: In cases where you are missing all of your natural teeth, dentures can be secured to several implants for a more secure and stable fit.
